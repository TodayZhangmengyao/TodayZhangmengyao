Analytical Modeling

Papers, textbooks, and notes that shaped my approach to formal modeling.

This list collects the analytical-modeling literature I keep returning to — game theory, mechanism design, information economics, and platform/market design. It is opinionated and incomplete; I add to it as I read. Suggestions are welcome: drop me a line at you@example.edu.

Entries marked foundational are required reading; survey entries give a broader map of a subfield; recent are papers from the last few years I find interesting.

I. Foundations & textbooks

Drew Fudenberg, Jean Tirole. Game Theory. MIT Press, 1991. foundational

The canonical graduate game theory reference. Chapters on dynamic games and repeated games are still the clearest exposition I know.

Roger B. Myerson. Game Theory: Analysis of Conflict. Harvard University Press, 1991. foundational

Complement to Fudenberg–Tirole; especially good on Bayesian games and mechanism design.

Jean Tirole. The Theory of Industrial Organization. MIT Press, 1988. foundational

Where I learned to model firms, pricing, and competition. Still the benchmark for clarity in IO.

II. Mechanism design & information economics

Roger B. Myerson. Optimal Auction Design. Mathematics of Operations Research, 6(1):58–73, 1981. foundational
DOI

The starting point for thinking about revenue-optimal mechanisms. Read once a year.

Sanford J. Grossman, Joseph E. Stiglitz. On the Impossibility of Informationally Efficient Markets. American Economic Review, 70(3):393–408, 1980. foundational

The intuition pump for why information acquisition matters in equilibrium.

III. Platforms & two-sided markets

Jean-Charles Rochet, Jean Tirole. Platform Competition in Two-Sided Markets. Journal of the European Economic Association, 1(4):990–1029, 2003. foundational

The paper that launched the modern platform literature.

Andrei Hagiu, Julian Wright. Multi-sided Platforms. International Journal of Industrial Organization, 43:162–174, 2015. survey

A clean taxonomy of platform business models, with the formal definitions I cite most often.
